# Carousel: "underrated COOL GIRL book recs"

## First impression
A dreamy scrapbook of literary-girl identity — every slide is someone's Pinterest board come to life, with hot pink threading through it as a signature.

## What this carousel is doing well
- **Templated rhythm with high-variance content.** Every body slide uses the same composition (polaroid frame + warm photo + book cover dropped center + italic tagline wrapping around the cover + small star + watermark + cursive corner footer), which lets the *photos* and *book covers* do all the visual heavy lifting. The viewer's eye learns the pattern after slide 2 and just absorbs content on slides 3-10.
- **Identity-callout hook that works without specificity.** "Cool girl" is broad, but the magenta-on-cream + mixed-serif typography + scrapbook collage on the cover signals exactly which "cool girl" — Babitz/Moshfegh/Cusk reading-femme literary girl. The aesthetic is the targeting.
- **Save-bait by structure.** Nine separate book recommendations means nine separate reasons to save. The reader doesn't need a save prompt; they save because they want to remember the list.

## What's weaker
- **No CTA on the final slide.** The carousel just ends on Rouge with no save/share/follow prompt and no loopback. Loses the easy save-rate boost a single line ("save this for your next bookstore run") would deliver. Save rate is probably already high from structure alone, but share/follow rate are leaving signal on the table.
- **Slide 1 collage is dense.** Glasses, doodles, coffee tray, sweater-and-bag photo, espresso pack, plus the headline — a lot competing for the eye at thumbnail size. The typography saves it, but a tighter cover would probably stop more thumbs.

## Replication notes (what I'd lift)
- **The polaroid-frame body template** with the small star + two-line all-caps watermark + cursive corner footers. It's a repeatable carousel system — once built as a Figma master, every new book rec is a 3-minute composite.
- **Mixed-serif display** (italic serif + black serif on the same line). Distinctive without being maximalist.
- **Hot pink as a sole accent through book-cover selection.** The carousel never declares "pink is my brand color" — it just consistently picks book covers that have pink in them, and the brand emerges from the curation. Smart.
- **Identity-callout + listicle hybrid** as the hook formula. "Underrated [identity] [things]" is a portable template.
